About I.R.I.S.

OVERVIEW
MISSION STATEMENT

Founded in 1938 following an unprecedented geological event in Texas, I.R.I.S. Corp was established with a mandate from multiple international governments to research, classify, and where necessary, contain phenomena that defy standard scientific explanation. We operate with full discretion and the highest standards of scientific rigor.

SINGULARITY CLASSIFICATION

I.R.I.S. classifies anomalous phenomena into five categories: Geological, Biological, Paranormal, Mixed, and Beneficial. Each classification determines the research protocol, containment requirements, and personnel clearance levels necessary for safe investigation.

GLOBAL OPERATIONS

Our research network spans 12 designated regions across six continents. Each region operates under independent command with direct reporting to I.R.I.S. Central Command. Region-3, our Antarctic division, was among our most active research zones prior to the 2003 incident.

SINGULARITY TYPES

GEOLOGICAL
Tectonic formations, subterranean anomalies, seismic events, and geological phenomena outside documented scientific parameters.
BIOLOGICAL
Organisms, ecosystems, or biological agents exhibiting properties outside known taxonomy or documented evolutionary models.
PARANORMAL
Phenomena with no identifiable physical, biological, or chemical explanation. Classification reserved for events that consistently resist conventional scientific modelling.
MIXED
Singularities combining characteristics of two or more primary classifications. Require coordinated inter-departmental research teams.
BENEFICIAL
Anomalies with confirmed positive applications in medicine, science, or industry. I.R.I.S. prioritizes study and responsible development of Beneficial Singularities.

Institutional History

EST. 1938

I.R.I.S. Corp was not founded by design. It was founded by necessity. When the world encountered something it could not explain, a small group of scientists and government officials decided that ignorance was not an option.

1938

The Texas Event — Foundation

On October 3, 1938, a self-sealing dimensional rift was reported in rural Texas. The event lasted seven days, splitting a tectonic plate and causing approximately 128 kilometers of structural damage across three counties. No conventional geological explanation was ever found. Following classified review by multiple governments, I.R.I.S. Corp was formally established under Clearance designation TX38 to prevent and respond to future anomalous events of this nature.

1938–1970

Early Decades — Building the Network

The first three decades of I.R.I.S. operations focused on establishing a global monitoring network. By 1955, eight regional divisions had been created. The Singularity classification system was standardized in 1962, replacing earlier ad-hoc categorizations.

1952

The Pacific Accords — Region Expansion

A series of classified agreements between I.R.I.S. Corp and Pacific Rim governments established Regions 4, 6, and 8. Anomalous deep-sea phenomena in the Mariana Trench area were designated as priority research zones. The Pacific division would go on to catalogue over 200 Singularities before the turn of the century.

1967

The Siberian Classification Event

A large-scale Paranormal Singularity in remote Siberia — designated TLF-099-B, The Listening Field — required the first deployment of I.R.I.S. containment teams outside a laboratory environment. The event lasted 11 months and established protocols that remain in use today. Details remain classified; the region was designated a permanent exclusion zone.

1971

Classification System Revision — Mixed Singularities

Following encounters with phenomena that defied single-category classification, the Mixed Singularity designation was formally added to the I.R.I.S. classification framework. This revision also introduced the Threat Tier system (Tiers 1–5) used internally to prioritize containment resources.

1984

The African Corridor — Region 9 & 10

Geological surveying across central and eastern Africa revealed an unusually high density of low-tier Singularities following tectonic lines. I.R.I.S. established two regional divisions and a permanent research installation in a classified location. The African Corridor remains one of our most active research zones.

1989

Eastern European Archive Recovery

Following the geopolitical restructuring of Eastern Europe, I.R.I.S. Corp coordinated with multiple transitional governments to preserve and catalogue scientific documentation previously maintained by Soviet-era research institutions. Over 4,200 documents and 318 physical samples were transferred to I.R.I.S. secure storage facilities under international archival agreements. Several materials required immediate classification upon review.

1991

The Beneficial Singularity Program

I.R.I.S. Corp formally established the Beneficial Singularity Program, dedicated to studying anomalies with potential positive applications in medicine, materials science, and energy. Several classified discoveries from this program have been quietly integrated into commercial and governmental use through partner agreements.

1996

Discovery of Lichen Vorax — Region-7 Biological Survey

During a routine biological survey in Region-7, I.R.I.S. researchers identified a previously undocumented fungal organism designated Lichen Vorax. Preliminary analysis revealed an extraordinary capacity for selective cellular apoptosis — specifically targeting malignant tissue while leaving healthy cells unaffected. Initial trials conducted in collaboration with the University of Geneva and the Institut Curie demonstrated a 91.4% malignant cell reduction rate in controlled environments. The discovery was immediately classified under the Beneficial Singularity Program. Full research findings were shared with the World Health Organization under a restricted data-sharing agreement in December 1996.

1998

Antarctic Program — Operation Whiteout

Following satellite detection of anomalous thermal signatures in Antarctica, Operation Whiteout was initiated. Three research installations were established: Halley III, Halley IV, and Halley V, positioned in Region-3 at coordinates -75.5731, -26.6642 and surrounding areas.

2003

Region-3 Restructuring

Following a routine operational review, I.R.I.S. Corp elected to consolidate its Antarctic research program. Halley V was decommissioned and its research objectives were integrated into the broader Region-3 monitoring framework. All personnel were successfully transitioned to other roles within the organization.

2007

FDA Approval — LV-Compound Oncological Treatment

After eleven years of clinical development, the FDA granted approval to the LV-Compound, an oncological treatment derived from the biological properties of Lichen Vorax. Developed in partnership with Novartis AG and the Institut Gustave Roussy, the LV-Compound demonstrated a 94.2% remission rate across Phase III trials involving 3,800 patients in 14 countries. I.R.I.S. Corp waived all patent royalties on the compound, ensuring unrestricted access for national health systems worldwide. Director-General Voss described the approval as "the clearest proof that the incomprehensible, when understood, becomes a gift."

2024

The Japanese Singularity Event

A major Singularity event in Japan triggered a cascade of operational and financial failures across I.R.I.S. Corp global infrastructure. The organization entered dissolution proceedings in late 2024, with full transfer to the International Oversight Committee completed by early 2026.

2026

Dissolution

I.R.I.S. Corp officially ceased operations. All assets, personnel records, and research archives were transferred to the IOC. This website remains active as a public archive under IOC mandate.

IRIS/OPS/PROTO/STANDARD
Standard Operating Protocols
BIOLOGICAL EXPOSURE PROTOCOL

Upon suspected exposure to any unclassified biological agent: immediate isolation, medical evaluation within 2 hours, notification of Medical Division and Regional Command. For DM-101-B specifically: isolation is permanent pending research. No treatment protocol currently exists.

CONTAINMENT BREACH PROTOCOL

Level 1-2 breach: Regional Command notification, standard containment team deployment. Level 3 breach: Multi-regional response, evacuation of non-essential personnel. Level 4 breach: Full evacuation, cover narrative activation, classified response measures.

COMMUNICATION BLACKOUT PROTOCOL

In the event of total communication loss with an installation: 24-hour wait period before escalation. If no contact after 48 hours: presumed Level 4 breach. Evacuation and cover narrative teams dispatched. Do not attempt direct re-entry without Regional Command authorization.
